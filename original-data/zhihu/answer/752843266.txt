<h3><b>Mixed division and multiplication</b></h3><p>Similarly, there can be ambiguity in the use of the <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slash_%28punctuation%29%23Arithmetic" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">slash symbol</a> / in expressions such as 1/2<i>x</i>.<a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_operations%23cite_note-Ball_1978-5" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">[5]</a> If one rewrites this expression as 1 ÷ 2<i>x</i>and then interprets the division symbol as indicating multiplication by the reciprocal, this becomes:</p><p>1 ÷ 2 ×<i>x</i>= 1 ×1/2×<i>x</i>=1/2×<i>x</i>.</p><p>With this interpretation 1 ÷ 2<i>x</i> is equal to (1 ÷ 2)<i>x</i>. However, in some of the academic literature, <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiplication_denoted_by_juxtaposition" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">multiplication denoted by juxtaposition</a> (also known as <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implied_multiplication" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">implied multiplication</a>) is interpreted as having higher precedence than division, so that 1 ÷ 2<i>x</i> equals 1 ÷ (2<i>x</i>), not (1 ÷ 2)<i>x</i>.</p><p>For example, the manuscript submission instructions for the <i><a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_Review" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Physical Review</a></i> journals state that multiplication is of higher precedence than division with a slash, and this is also the convention observed in prominent physics textbooks such as the <i><a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Course_of_Theoretical_Physics" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Course of Theoretical Physics</a></i> by <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lev_Landau" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Landau</a> and <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evgeny_Lifshitz" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Lifshitz</a> and the <i><a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feynman_Lectures_on_Physics" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Feynman Lectures on
